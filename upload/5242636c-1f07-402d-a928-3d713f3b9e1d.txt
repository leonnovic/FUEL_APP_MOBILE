# Actual Revenue Inflows vs. Borrowing/Debt Transactions
## PUBLICAN ENERGY M-PESA Merchant Account Analysis

Based on my review of all provided M-PESA statements, here is the classification of transaction types:

---

## ✅ ACTUAL INFLOWS (Genuine Revenue - NOT Borrowing/Debt)

### Primary Revenue Transaction Types:
| Transaction Type | Details Pattern | Description |
|-----------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Customer Merchant Payment** | `Merchant Payment from [phone]- [Customer Name]` | Direct customer payments for goods/services |
| **Customer Merchant Payment** | `Merchant Payment Online from [phone]- [Customer Name]` | Online/digital customer payments |
| **Customer Merchant Payment** | `Small Business Pay Merchant from [phone]- [Customer Name]` | Small business customer payments |
| **Organization To Organization Transfer** | `Business Buy Goods by [Till] - [Service] via API...` | B2B customer purchases (e.g., Bonga Everywhere, CO-OP TO TILL) |

### Key Characteristics of Genuine Inflows:
- ✅ "Paid In" column shows positive amounts
- ✅ Counterparty is a customer (individual or business)
- ✅ Description includes customer name/phone
- ✅ No mention of "Boost," "Overdraft," "Loan," or "OD"
- ✅ Transaction Type = "Customer Merchant Payment" or legitimate B2B transfer

---

## ❌ TRANSACTIONS TO EXCLUDE (Borrowing/Debt or Non-Revenue)

### Overdraft/Loan-Related (NOT Revenue):
| Transaction Type | Details Pattern | Why Exclude |
|-----------------|----------------|-------------|
| OD Payment Transfer | `Loan Disbursement from 804080- Boost ya Biashara` | **Borrowed funds** - must be repaid |
| OD Payment Transfer | `Biashara Overdraft Repayment` | **Debt repayment** - cash outflow |
| OD Payment Transfer | `Merchant Pay Utility With Biashara Overdraft...` | Using borrowed money to pay bills |
| OD Payment Transfer | `Merchant to Utility Payment Charge to M-PESA` | Fee for overdraft usage |

### Other Non-Revenue Items:
| Transaction Type | Details Pattern | Why Exclude |
|-----------------|----------------|-------------|
| Merchant Customer Payment | `Merchant Customer Payment to [phone]- [Name]` | **Outflow** - paying customers/refunds |
| Pay merchant Charge | `Pay merchant Charge` | **Fee expense** - M-PESA service charges |
| Merchant to Merchant Payment Charge | `Merchant to Merchant Payment Charge to M-PESA` | **Fee expense** for inter-merchant transfers |
| Organization To Organization Transfer | `Merchant Pay Utility to 522522-KCB Paybill...` | **Bill payment outflow** |

## 📊 Quick Reference: How to Identify Genuine Inflows

When reviewing statements, look for these **GREEN FLAGS**:
```
✅ Transaction Type: "Customer Merchant Payment"
✅ Details start with: "Merchant Payment from..." or "Merchant Payment Online from..."
✅ "Paid In" has a value; "Withdrawn" = 0.00
✅ Other Party: "578590-PUBLICAN ENERGY" (your till receiving money)
✅ No keywords: "Boost", "Overdraft", "Loan", "OD", "Repayment"
```

**RED FLAGS** (exclude these):
```
❌ Transaction Type: "OD Payment Transfer"
❌ Details contain: "Boost ya Biashara", "Loan Disbursement", "Overdraft Repayment"
❌ Details contain: "Merchant Pay Utility With Biashara Overdraft"
❌ Transaction Type: "Merchant Customer Payment" (this is money going OUT)
❌ Details contain: "Pay merchant Charge" (this is a fee)
```

---

## 💡 Practical Tip for Accounting

When compiling revenue reports:
1. **Filter** for `Transaction Type = "Customer Merchant Payment"`
2. **Sum** the "Paid In" column for these rows only
3. **Exclude** all rows with "OD Payment Transfer" or "Boost ya Biashara"
4. **Separately track** "Pay merchant Charge" as operating expenses
5. **Reconcile** "Biashara Overdraft Repayment" against loan liability accounts

This ensures your revenue figures reflect actual sales, not borrowed funds that create future repayment obligations.
